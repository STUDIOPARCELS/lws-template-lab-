05 · NARRATIVE — TEXT FIRSTtext-first
Flipped
A minimalist photographic work that renders a flipped iceberg as the visible inversion of class power: blue rises, white sinks.

AI, robotics, and accelerated automation are redrawing social order. Blue-collar work gains status and leverage while routine white-collar roles compress. The iceberg becomes a clean grammar for that shift. Traditional ice shows white above and deep blue below. A flipped iceberg reverses those planes. The surface turns blue. White drops under the line.

FLIPPED reads the present and projects the near future: AI and automation shift power from routine white-collar administration to blue work—build, repair, operate. LISA WOOD STUDIO grounds the argument in two sources: medium-format photographs from two Greenland expeditions and AI images authored through precise prompts. The pair creates a single visual grammar: a flipped iceberg where deep blue rises above the line and white drops below it. Ice and light carry the claim without rhetoric. The sequence lands the inversion in one clean beat and holds on the new order.
